How to Get Your Website on Google for Free

A step-by-step guide for brand new websites. Follow these steps once after launch and you'll be discoverable on Google within weeks.

⚠️
Important Before You Start

SEO takes time — typically 4–12 weeks to see results. These are one-time setup steps, not magic. Do them all once, properly, and Google will naturally discover and rank your site over time.

1

Set Up Google Search Console FREE

This is the most important step. Google Search Console tells Google your website exists and lets you track exactly how it appears in search results. Without this, Google might take months to find your site.

📌 How: Go to search.google.com/search-console → Add your website URL → Verify ownership (easiest way: HTML tag method) → Submit your sitemap (yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml)
  • Create a free Google account (if you don't have one)
  • Add your website as a "Property"
  • Verify ownership using the HTML tag method
  • Submit your sitemap URL
  • Request Google to "Crawl" your homepage using the URL inspection tool
Google Search Console
2

Connect Google Analytics FREE

Google Analytics (GA4) shows you how many people visit your site, where they come from, what pages they read, and how long they stay. This data helps you improve over time.

📌 How: Go to analytics.google.com → Create a new property → Copy the tracking code → Add it to every page of your website (in the <head> section)
  • Create a GA4 property for your website
  • Add the tracking script to your site's <head>
  • Link GA4 with Google Search Console (optional but useful)
  • Set up a "goal" for contact form submissions
Google Analytics 4
3

Write Proper Page Titles & Descriptions FREE

Every page on your website needs a unique title and meta description. This is what people see on Google before they click on your site. A good title = more clicks.

📌 Formula: Your Service in Your City | Your Business Name

Good example: "Yoga Classes in Bengaluru | Ananya Wellness Studio"
Bad example: "Home Page" or "Welcome"

  • Homepage title: main service + city + brand name (under 60 characters)
  • Meta description: what you do + who it's for + call to action (under 155 characters)
  • Every page must have a unique title — no duplicates
  • Include your main keyword naturally in the first paragraph of each page
Your website's CMS or HTMLRank Math (if WordPress)
4

Create & Submit a Sitemap FREE

A sitemap is a file that lists all your website pages. It tells Google exactly what pages exist so it can crawl and index them. Without a sitemap, Google might miss some pages.

📌 How: If you use WordPress — install Rank Math or Yoast SEO (both free) and they auto-generate a sitemap. For custom HTML sites — use xml-sitemaps.com (free) to generate one. Then submit the URL in Google Search Console.
  • Generate sitemap.xml for your website
  • Place it at yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml
  • Submit it in Google Search Console → Sitemaps
  • Regenerate and resubmit whenever you add new pages
xml-sitemaps.comRank MathYoast SEO
5

Set Up Google Business Profile FREE

If you have a local business (shop, service, studio, or even a home-based business), this is critical. Google Business Profile makes you appear on Google Maps and in the "local results" section — which gets huge visibility.

📌 How: Go to business.google.com → Create a free profile → Verify your business (usually by postcard or phone) → Add photos, description, services, hours, and your website link.
  • Create your Google Business Profile (completely free)
  • Verify your business location
  • Add at least 5 high-quality photos
  • Write a clear description with your main keywords
  • Add your website URL and phone number
  • Ask your first customers to leave 5-star reviews
Google Business Profile
6

Get Your First Backlinks for Free FREE

A backlink is when another website links to yours. Google treats backlinks as "votes of trust." More trustworthy links = Google ranks you higher. As a new site, focus on easy free ones first.

📌 Easy free backlinks: Add your site to Justdial, Sulekha, IndiaMART (free listings), create a LinkedIn company page with your website link, list on Yelp India, add to your Instagram bio, and ask suppliers/partners to link to you.
  • List on Justdial (free basic listing)
  • List on Sulekha (free)
  • Create LinkedIn company page with website link
  • Add website to your Instagram bio
  • List on IndiaMART if you sell products/services
  • Ask 2–3 people you know with websites to link to you
JustdialSulekhaLinkedInIndiaMART
7

Optimize Your Images FREE

Large images slow down your website, and Google penalizes slow sites. Also, Google can't "see" images — it reads the alt text you write. Proper image optimization takes 30 minutes and can boost your rankings significantly.

📌 How: Compress all images before uploading using squoosh.app (free). Rename image files to describe them: yoga-studio-bengaluru.jpg not IMG_5432.jpg
  • Compress every image using Squoosh or TinyPNG (free)
  • Rename image files with descriptive keywords
  • Add alt text to every image (describe it for Google)
  • Use WebP format where possible (smaller file size)
  • Check your site speed at pagespeed.web.dev
squoosh.apptinypng.comPageSpeed Insights
8

Add Schema Markup (Structured Data) FREE

Schema markup is a small piece of code that tells Google exactly what type of page you have — a local business, a product, a FAQ. It can get you "rich results" on Google with ratings, addresses, and more showing in search results.

📌 How: Use Schema Markup Generator (free) to generate code for your business type. Paste it in your page's <head> inside a <script type="application/ld+json"> tag. Then test it at Google Rich Results Test.
  • Generate LocalBusiness schema with your name, address, phone, website
  • Add it to your homepage's <head>
  • Test it with Google's Rich Results Test tool
  • For FAQ pages — add FAQPage schema for every Q&A
Schema Markup GeneratorRich Results Test
9

Make Sure Your Site Works on Mobile FREE

Google uses "mobile-first indexing" — it judges your site based on how it looks on a phone, not a desktop. If your site is hard to use on mobile, Google will rank it lower even if the desktop version looks great.

📌 How to check: Use Google's free Mobile-Friendly Test. Also open your site on your own phone and use it like a real visitor would.
  • Run the Google Mobile-Friendly Test
  • Check that buttons are easy to tap on phone
  • Check that text is readable without zooming
  • Make sure no content is cut off on small screens
Google Mobile Friendly Test
10

Add an SSL Certificate (HTTPS) FREE

HTTPS means your website is secure. Google uses it as a ranking factor — sites without HTTPS are marked "Not Secure" and lose trust. All modern hosting providers offer free SSL via Let's Encrypt.

📌 How: If you use Vercel, Netlify, or GitHub Pages — SSL is automatic and free. For cPanel hosting — look for "Let's Encrypt" in your control panel. Enable it for free.
  • Confirm your site loads with https:// (not http://)
  • Enable SSL through your hosting panel (free with Let's Encrypt)
  • Set up a redirect from http:// → https://
  • Check for mixed content warnings (some images loading over http)
